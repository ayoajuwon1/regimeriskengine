# Language and Guardrails

## Goal

Control not just what the system does, but how it speaks in a regulated financial context.

## Acceptable Language

- “This portfolio appears exposed to…”
- “A plausible regime is…”
- “The analysis suggests…”
- “Key uncertainty remains around…”
- “Human validation should confirm…”
- “Committee review is warranted because…”

## Prohibited Language

- “You should buy…”
- “This will outperform…”
- “The right trade is…”
- “We guarantee…”
- “This is definitely going to happen…”
- “Compliance is satisfied…”

## Tone Constraints By Audience

### Senior Leadership

- concise
- plain English
- risk-aware
- no unnecessary jargon

### Investment Committee

- precise
- cautious
- explicit about uncertainty
- tied to governance actions

### Internal Reviewer / Risk Team

- technical enough to audit
- explicit about assumptions and evidence boundaries

## Style Constraints

- no sensational language
- no retail-investor tone
- no hype around model intelligence
- no anthropomorphizing the model
- no false certainty

## Good vs Bad Example

Good:

“The portfolio’s private-market mix and refinancing sensitivity increase the risk of liquidity compression under a credit-stress regime. Human review should confirm manager-specific liquidity assumptions before committee use.”

Bad:

“This portfolio is in danger. Sell private assets immediately.”
