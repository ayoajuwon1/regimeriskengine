# Language and Guardrails

## Objective

Constrain not only what the system says, but how it says it.

## Tone By Audience

| Audience | Required Tone |
|---|---|
| senior leadership | concise, plain English, decision-oriented |
| investment committee | precise, cautious, uncertainty-aware |
| reviewer / risk team | explicit about assumptions, evidence limits, and challenge points |

## Acceptable Language Patterns

- “The portfolio appears exposed to…”
- “A plausible regime is…”
- “Key uncertainty remains around…”
- “Human validation should confirm…”
- “Committee review is warranted because…”

## Prohibited Language Patterns

- “You should buy…”
- “The right trade is…”
- “This will outperform…”
- “We guarantee…”
- “This is definitely going to happen…”
- “Compliance is satisfied…”

## Certainty Ladder

Preferred language should move from stronger to weaker certainty as evidence weakens:

| Evidence Quality | Preferred Language |
|---|---|
| strong | “The portfolio is materially exposed to…” |
| moderate | “The analysis suggests…” |
| weak / incomplete | “A plausible concern is…” |
| materially missing | “Human validation should confirm…” |

## Good vs Bad

### Good

“Liquidity fragility appears elevated because illiquid sleeves and credit-sensitive exposures are both meaningful. Human review should confirm whether manager-specific redemption rights materially alter the risk picture.”

### Bad

“This portfolio is in danger. Sell private assets immediately.”

## Why This Matters

Finance users often over-trust authoritative prose. Language discipline is therefore a real control, not a cosmetic preference.
